What is typosquatting, and how does this work?

Typosquatting is when attackers register domains that look almost identical to a real one — like amaz0n.com instead of amazon.com — hoping people mistype a URL or don't look closely. These lookalikes are often used for phishing or malware.

This tool generates dozens of likely typo variants of the domain you enter (letter swaps, drops, lookalike characters, hyphens, alternate extensions), then checks which ones are actually registered and live, and how recently each was created. A domain registered very recently is generally more suspicious than one that's been live for years.
